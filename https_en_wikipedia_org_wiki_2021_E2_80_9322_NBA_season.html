The 2021–22 NBA season will be the 76th season of the National Basketball Association (NBA). The NBA returned to a full 82-game regular season in its normal mid-October to mid-April schedule. The regular season will begin on October 19, 2021.[1] The 2022 NBA All-Star Game will be played at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland on February 20, 2022.

75th anniversary promotions[edit]

On July 7, 2021, the NBA announced that it would commemorate its 75th anniversary throughout the 2021–22 season. A 75th anniversary diamond logo will be featured in promotions across all NBA properties during the season, including on merchandise, digital and social media, and painted on the courts.[2][3] The NBA also announced that they would release a list of the 75 greatest players in league history chosen by a panel of media, current and former players, coaches, general managers, and team executives.[4]

Main article: List of 2021–22 NBA season transactions

Retirement[edit]

  • On July 6, 2021, Ian Mahinmi announced his retirement from the NBA. Mahinmi played 12 seasons in the NBA, winning one championship with the Dallas Mavericks in 2011.[5]
  • On July 18, 2021, Omri Casspi announced his retirement from the NBA. Casspi played for seven teams during his 10-year NBA career.[6]
  • On July 21, 2021, Amile Jefferson announced his retirement from the NBA. Jefferson played two seasons with the Orlando Magic.[7]
  • On August 7, 2021, Jarrett Jack announced his retirement from the NBA. Jack played for nine teams during his 13-year NBA career. [8]
  • On August 12, 2021, Kyle Korver announced his retirement from the NBA. Korver played for six teams during his 17-year NBA career.[9]
  • On August 24, 2021, Jared Dudley announced his retirement from the NBA. Dudley played for seven teams during his 14-year NBA career, winning an NBA championship with the Los Angeles Lakers in 2020.[10]

Free agency[edit]

Free agency began on August 2, 2021. Notable signings included longtime Toronto Raptors guard Kyle Lowry announcing his decision to sign with Miami Heat on a long term deal. Lonzo Ball announced he would be signing a four-year, $85 million dollar deal with the Chicago Bulls, joining fellow free agency signing DeMar DeRozan. The Los Angeles Clippers re-signed superstar Kawhi Leonard, and the Phoenix Suns re-signed superstar and former Clipper Chris Paul.

Off-season[edit]

  • On June 2, 2021, Boston Celtics head coach Brad Stevens was promoted to president of basketball operations following the retirement of Danny Ainge.[11]
  • On June 4, 2021, the Portland Trail Blazers and Terry Stotts had mutually agreed to part ways after nine seasons with the team.[12]
  • On June 5, 2021, the Orlando Magic and Steve Clifford had mutually agreed to part ways after three seasons with the team.[13]
  • On June 9, 2021, the Indiana Pacers fired head coach Nate Bjorkgren after only one season with the team in which they missed the playoffs for the first time in six years.[14][15]
  • On June 16, 2021, the New Orleans Pelicans and head coach Stan Van Gundy had mutually agreed to part ways after only one season.[16]
  • On June 16, 2021, the Washington Wizards and head coach Scott Brooks part ways after five seasons with the team.[17]
  • On June 17, 2021, Rick Carlisle resigned from his position as head coach of the Dallas Mavericks after the 13 seasons with the team.[18][19]
  • On June 24, 2021, the Indiana Pacers hired Rick Carlisle as their new head coach.[20]
  • On June 27, 2021, the Portland Trail Blazers hired Chauncey Billups as their new head coach.[21]
  • On June 28, 2021, the Boston Celtics hired Ime Udoka as their new head coach.[22]
  • On June 28, 2021, the Dallas Mavericks hired Jason Kidd as their new head coach.[23]
  • On July 7, 2021, the Atlanta Hawks hired Nate McMillan as full-time head coach.[24]
  • On July 11, 2021, the Orlando Magic hired Jamahl Mosley as their new head coach.[25]
  • On July 17, 2021, the Washington Wizards hired Wes Unseld Jr. as their new head coach.[26]
  • On July 22, 2021, the New Orleans Pelicans hired Willie Green as their new head coach.[27]

Preseason[edit]

The preseason will begin on October 3, 2021.[28]

Regular season[edit]

The regular season will begin on October 19, 2021, and will end on April 10, 2022, returning to its normal schedule after the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted the schedule during the previous two seasons.[1]

Play-in tournament[edit]

The NBA will stage a play-in tournament for teams ranked 7th through 10th in each conference from April 12-15, 2022. The 7th place team will host the 8th place team with the winner clinching the 7th seed in the playoffs. The 9th place team will host the 10th place team with the loser being eliminated from playoff contention. The 7th–8th place game's loser will then host the 9th–10th place game's winner, with the winner clinching the 8th seed and the loser being eliminated.[29]

Playoffs[edit]

The playoffs will begin on April 16, 2022. The Finals are set to begin on June 2, with a potential Game 7 scheduled for June 19.[1]

Arenas[edit]

  • The Miami Heat's home arena, formerly known as American Airlines Arena, was renamed FTX Arena on June 4, 2021.[30][31]
  • The Phoenix Suns' home arena, formerly known as Phoenix Suns Arena, was renamed Footprint Center on July 16, 2021.[32]
  • The Oklahoma City Thunder's home arena, formerly known as Chesapeake Energy Arena, was renamed Paycom Center on July 27, 2021.[33]

Media[edit]

This will be the sixth year of a nine-year deal with ABC, ESPN, TNT and NBA TV.[34]

The Portland Trail Blazers signed a broadcasting deal with Root Sports Northwest, replacing NBC Sports Northwest as the team's game broadcasters.[35]

Notable occurrences[edit]

  • This will be the first season for Wilson as manufacturer and supplier of official NBA game balls again, replacing Spalding. Wilson's initial partnership with the league began in 1946 under the NBA's predecessor Basketball Association of America (BAA) until it ended after 37 seasons in 1983, when Spalding took over.[36][37]
