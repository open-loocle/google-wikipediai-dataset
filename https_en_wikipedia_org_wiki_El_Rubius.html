Rubén Doblas Gundersen (Spanish: [ruˈβen ˈdoβlaz ˈɣundeɾsen]; [a] born 13 February 1990 in Mijas), better known by his pseudonym El Rubius or elrubiusOMG (/ˈruːbiəs/), is a Spanish-Norwegian YouTube personality whose channel primarily consists of gameplays and vlogs. His channel currently has over 9.1 billion views[5][6] and 40 million subscribers.[7] It is also the 50th most subscribed YouTube channel in the world.

A tweet from Rubius's official Twitter account was the most retweeted tweet in the world for the year 2016. The tweet was retweeted more than 1.3 million times.[8]

He is one of the most popular streamers on Twitch, having the third most-followed Twitch channel in the world.[9][10] In January 2021 he changed his place of residence from Spain to Andorra.[11]

Early years and education[edit]

Rubén Doblas Gundersen was born on February 13, 1990, in the town of Mijas, Málaga.[12]Son of a Norwegian mother, named Bente, and a Spanish father, he has a younger sister named Liv on his mother's side and two brothers on his father's side.[13]When he was 3 years old, his parents divorced. His mother moved to Bergen with Rubén, where she again became a partner with another Spanish man named Héctor, whom Rubius often refers to as The Godfather in his videos. Because of that, he moved back to Madrid, where he went through primary and secondary school. His first console was a Super NES, to which Rubén admitted that it was essential to develop his love for video games and geek culture, and that if it hadn't been for that he would never have reached where he was.[14][15]

In his high school stage, at the age of 16 his parents separated again, so he relocated to Norway again. During this time he met Miguel Ángel Rogel Ruíz, better known by his online alias mangelrogel or simply Mangel, through an internet forum, and began to upload his first videos on YouTube through his user elrubius, which Rubén claimed to have chosen that as a play on words since as a child he had blonde hair and his name was Rubén. He finished his studies and returned to Spain with his Godfather, where he worked as an intern and in a supermarket before entering to study 3D modeling and animation in Arteneo school in Madrid.[13]

Career[edit]

YouTube channel[edit]

El Rubius has two channels: elrubiusOMG and Rubius Z. His first channel "elrubiusOMG" was created on April 3, 2006. His second channel "Rubius Z" was created on December 19, 2011 because he could not become a YouTube partner on his first channel due to a strike.

Although his channels are focused on gameplays, El Rubius also posts vlogs, challenges, answers fan questions and does sketch comedies, among others.

In 2011, his channel started becoming popular in many Spanish-speaking countries. He gained attention when he posted a gameplay of The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim.[16] He later published videos of him on the Chatroulette website, which gained him an even larger following.[17]

He became the most subscribed Spanish channel in November 2012, surpassing fellow YouTuber Willyrex.[18] In February 2013, he reached 1 million subscribers and two years later, in February 2015, he became the first Spanish YouTuber whose channel reached 10 million subscribers,[19] and in July 2018 he reached 30 million subscribers.[20] Therefore, in October 2015, he got the Diamond Play Button at the YouTube 10th Anniversary Party in Madrid.[21][22]

El Rubius has made appearances in the 2015 and 2016 editions of YouTube Rewind.[23][24]

On March 25, 2018 elrubiusOMG broke Ninja and Drake's record for most concurrent viewers during a Fortnite stream and the record for most concurrent viewers on a YouTube Gaming livestream with 1.1 million people tuning in.[25] The tournament consisted of 99 other Spanish-speaking streamers who all broadcast the game to their own audiences, all together the Battle Royale tournament had over 42M live views.[26]

Publications[edit]

In 2014, El Rubius published El libro Troll, an interactive book full of challenges.[27] The book reached number one in sales in Spain for eight weeks and was a bestseller at the Madrid Book Fair 2014.[28] The Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio deemed it unsuitable for children and teenagers,[29] and it was described by parents as a sexual book.[30]

Spanish publishing group Planeta published his first comic entitled El Rubius: Virtual Hero on September 15, 2015.[31] Its second part, whose title is Virtual Hero II: La torre imposible, was published in June 2016.[32] The third and last part of the saga was published on April 4, 2017, and it was entitled Virtual Hero III: La máscara del troll.[33]

On 3 October 2018 he uploaded a documentary about his life on his YouTube channel.[34] It was released on Movistar+ in October 11, 2018.[35] On 12 October 2018, Virtual Hero, the first anime series released in Spain, premiered on Movistar+.[36][37] It is directed by Alexis Barroso, illustrated by Lolita Aldea and written by Juan Torres, based upon the comic by El Rubius.[38]

Awards[edit]

On April 21, 2016, El Rubius received the "YouTuber of the Year" award at the 1st Play Awards ceremony celebrated in Palma de Mallorca, Balearic Islands, Spain.[39]
