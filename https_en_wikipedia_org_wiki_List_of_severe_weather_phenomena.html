Examples[edit]

Severe weather can occur under a variety of situations, but three characteristics are generally needed: a temperature or moisture boundary, moisture, and (in the event of severe, precipitation-based events) instability in the atmosphere.

  • Cold wave
  • Cyclone (generic)
  • Medicane
    • Arctic cyclone
  • Fog
    • Ice fog
  • bomb Cyclone
  • gale
    • Sea fret

  • shelf cloud
  • gust front

    • Squall line
  • Tornado (also colloquially referred to as a "whirlwind" or "twister")

Phenomena caused by severe thunderstorms[edit]

  • Large Hail
  • High winds – 93 km/h(58 mph) or higher.
  • Tornadoes
  • Deadly Lightning
  • Flood, flash flood coastal flooding
  • thunder snow snow squalls
  • heavy rain (5 inches or greater)
