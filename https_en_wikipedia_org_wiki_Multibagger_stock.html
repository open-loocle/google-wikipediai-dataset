Multibagger stock

A multibagger stock is an equity stock which gives a return of more than 100%.[1] The term was coined by Peter Lynch in his 1988 book One Up on Wall Street and comes from baseball where "bags" or "bases" that a runner reaches are the measure of the success of a play.[2] For example, a ten bagger is a stock which gives returns equal to 10 times the investment, while a twenty bagger stock gives a return of 20 times.[3]

This term is especially common when discussing high-growth industries and emerging markets such as the BRICS.[4] As with most investment metrics, past performance is no guarantee of future returns, and multibag returns may be indicative of either sustained growth or an investment bubble.

Examples[edit]

Examples of multibagger stocks on the NASDAQ in 2015 were:

  • Energy Focus Inc: A return of more than 1700% in the last 2 years. (as on 16 October 2015)[5]
  • EBIX Inc: A return of more than 200% in the last 2 years. (as on 16 October 2015)[6]
  • Expedia Inc: A return of more than 200% in the last 2 years. (as on 16 October 2015)[7]
  • Netflix Inc: A return of more than 200% in the last 2 years. (as on 16 October 2015)[8]

Examples of multibagger stocks in India in 2015 were:

  • Uniply Industries: A return of more than 1400% in the last 1 year. (as on 24 September 2015)[9]
  • Mangalam Drugs: A return of more than 400% in the last 1 year. (as on 24 September 2015)[9]
  • Intrasoft Technologies: A return of more than 600% in the last 1 year. (as on 24 September 2015)[9]
  • Lanco Industries: A return of more than 400% in the last 1 year. (as on 24 September 2015)[9]
  • Shreyas Shipping & Logistic: A return of more than 500% in the last 1 year. (as on 24 September 2015)[9]
  • Symphony Limited: A return of more than 9,000% in the last 10 years. (as on December 5, 2016)[10]
