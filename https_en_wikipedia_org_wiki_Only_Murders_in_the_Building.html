Only Murders in the Building is an American mystery-comedy television series, created by Steve Martin and John Hoffman. The series, consisting of ten episodes, premiered on August 31, 2021 on Hulu.[1][2][3] Only Murders in the Building follows three strangers, played by Steve Martin, Martin Short, and Selena Gomez, who share an obsession with a true crime podcast.[4][5] After a murder occurs in their building, the three neighbors decide to start their own show to follow their investigation. Only Murders in the Building received largely positive reviews from critics, who praised its comedic approach to crime fiction and the three leads' performances.

Cast and characters[edit]

Main[edit]

  • Steve Martin as Charles-Haden Savage,[6] an actor who became famous as the star of a 1980s detective drama before drifting into obscurity. He lives in the Arconia.
  • Martin Short as Oliver Putnam,[6] a struggling Broadway director who spawns the idea of the podcast, and becomes its director. He lives in the Arconia.
  • Selena Gomez as Mabel Mora,[6] a young woman who lives in an unfinished apartment in the Arconia she claims to be renovating for her aunt
  • Aaron Dominguez as Oscar, a former close friend of Mabel and Tim, who was convicted of the murder of their friend Zoe
  • Amy Ryan as Jan, a professional bassoonist who also lives in the Arconia

Recurring[edit]

  • Vanessa Aspillaga as Ursula, the Arconia building manager
  • Julian Cihi as Tim Kono, a recently deceased resident in the Arconia building. Tim Kono's death under mysterious circumstances spurs Charles, Oliver, and Mabel to do their own investigation.
  • Jackie Hoffman as a neighbor
  • Jayne Houdyshell as Bunny, a board member and resident of the Arconia building
  • Nathan Lane as Teddy Dimas, an Arconia resident, deli chain owner, and old friend of Oliver who formerly produced many of Oliver's shows. He agrees to sponsor the podcast out of pity.

Guest[edit]

  • Tina Fey as Cinda Canning, the host of a true crime podcast called "All is Not OK in Oklahoma"[7]
  • Maulik Pancholy as Arnav, Charles's next door neighbor
  • Sting as himself,[8] a resident of the Arconia
  • Adriane Lenox as Roberta, Oliver's ex-wife

Production[edit]

Development[edit]

In January 2020, it was announced Steve Martin and Martin Short would star in an untitled Hulu series, created by Martin and John Hoffman, with Martin, Short, and Hoffman set to executive produce, alongside Dan Fogelman, with 20th Television serving as the studio.[10]

Casting[edit]

Alongside the initial announcement, it was announced Martin and Short would star in the series.[10] In August 2020, Selena Gomez joined the cast and also serves as an executive producer.[11] In November 2020, Aaron Dominguez joined the cast in a series regular role.[12] In January 2021, Amy Ryan joined the cast in a series regular role.[13] That same month, Nathan Lane joined the cast in a recurring role.[14]

Filming[edit]

Principal photography began on December 3, 2020, in New York City,[15] and concluded in April 2021.[16]

Release[edit]

Only Murders in the Building premiered on August 31, 2021 on Hulu.[3] Internationally, the series premiered on the same day on Disney+ under the dedicated streaming hub Star, as an original series, and on Star+ in Latin America. Disney+ Hotstar released the series in select territories on September 3, 2021.[17] On Disney+, Disney+ Hotstar, and Star+, Only Murders in the Building episodes is scheduled to debut on a weekly basis.[2]

Reception[edit]

The performances of (left to right) Steve Martin, Martin Short, and Selena Gomez received critical acclaim.

The review aggregator website Rotten Tomatoes reported a 100% approval rating with an average rating of 7.80/10, based on 45 critic reviews. The website's critics consensus reads, "Only Murders in the Building's silly approach to true crime obsessives is at once hilarious and insightful, thanks in large part to its extremely charming central trio."[18] Metacritic gave the series a weighted average score of 76 out of 100 based on 32 critic reviews, indicating "generally favorable reviews".[19]

Kristen Baldwin of Entertainment Weekly gave the series a B and wrote, "Only Murders delivers above-average laughs, a clever mystery, and a starry cast. But it's hard to shake the feeling that the show missed an opportunity to be something special."[20] Reviewing the series for Rolling Stone, Alan Sepinwall gave a rating of four and a half out of five stars and said, "The series soon turns out to be—like The Princess Bride, Galaxy Quest, or Jane the Virgin—that rare and wonderful thing: the parody that also offers a great example of the genuine article."[21]
