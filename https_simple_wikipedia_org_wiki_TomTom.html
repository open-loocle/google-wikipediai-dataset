TomTom N.V. is a Dutch company that provides devices for car and personal GPS navigation. Its production is famous and actively used in Europe, North America and some Asian countries.[2]

History[change | change source]

TomTom was founded in 1991.

In September 2005, TomTom bought Datafactory AG.[3]

In 2006, TomTom Traffic was created after buying Applied Generics.[4]

In 2008, the company owned Tele Atlas.[5]

In 2012, TomTom was the main to provide mapping data for Apple's iOS 6 Maps.[6]

In 2014, the company was a partner with Volkswagen Group during the research on Highly Automated Driving (HAD) systems.[7] TomTom N.V. provided Volkswagen Group, Mercedes, Toyota and other big carmakers with their navigation devices.[8]

In 2015, Uber started using TomTom's maps and traffic data in 300 cities.[9]

In 2020, Huawei replaced Google Maps with TomTom's maps on their smartphones.[10]

Structure[change | change source]

TomTom is oriented on 2 main market segments: location technology and consumer.[11]

Location technology refers to the company's automotive and enterprise businesses. The company provides cars, tech specialists, some companies and government bodies with maps and navigation software.[11]

The consumer segment is related to selling portable, personal satellite navigation devices.[12] Recently TomTom offered software applications with digital maplinked services instead of devices.[13]

Products and services[change | change source]

TomTom N.V. offers three types of products: maps, connected services and (navigation) software. The company sells Navigation devices (PNDs) and TomTom GO navigation apps.[14]

A TomTom's traffic monitoring service uses different sources to give traffic information by combining data from:

  • traditional sources: governmental/third-party data such as induction loops in the roads, cameras and traffic surveillance
  • new sources: traffic flow of millions of anonymous mobile phone users.[15]
