Amazon Prime Video: How to get a Prime Video free trial

You’ve probably heard of Amazon Prime and its many online shopping benefits, but Amazon Prime Video may be new to your ears. Amazon’s exclusive streaming service is the place to go for award-winning original shows, binge-worthy box sets, and blockbuster movies. Of course, it’s hard to know if it’s worth paying for any service without trying it. Luckily, Prime Video has a few free trials and deals to take advantage of. Read on for how to get them. 

What is Amazon Prime Video? 

Amazon Prime Video is Amazon’s on-demand streaming service. Existing Prime members can enjoy the service for free, but non-Prime users can subscribe to Prime Video separately if preferred. You’ll find thousands of titles to watch, from action-packed flicks to highly entertaining reality shows. Plus, you can’t miss Prime Originals. Whether it’s The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel or Tom Clancy’s Jack Ryan series, Amazon’s exclusive shows are a must-watch.

For an additional monthly cost, you can subscribe to over 100 networks for even more on-demand content and, in some cases, the ability to watch live TV — premium names like HBO and Showtime feature on the list. There’s also the opportunity to rent and buy tons of shows and movies.

How do I sign up for an Amazon Prime Video free trial?

Signing up for a Prime Video free trial is pretty straightforward because there’s only one way to do it. First, go to the Amazon website. At the top of the screen, you’ll see a button asking you to Try Prime. 

If you have an Amazon account, you’ll be prompted to sign in. But if you’re new to the service, you can create an account. Note that you will have to give over a few personal details like your email address and card payment information.

Once you’re signed in, click Start Your Trial. You’ll be taken to another page and asked to click Start Your Free Trial. Select that button and your free trial will begin.

What types of free trials does Amazon Prime Video typically offer?

There isn’t a separate Amazon Video trial. But Amazon offers a whopping 30-day free trial of its full Prime service for new customers or people who haven’t tried Prime in a while. This 30-day offer is available all year round.

College students are the only people who can enjoy an extended free trial. They’ll receive six months for free — a deal that’s pretty hard to turn down.

Are there bundle deals with Amazon Prime Video?

Unfortunately, Prime Video doesn’t form part of a streaming service bundle. You can’t buy it as an add-on to another service, either. The only way to get Prime Video is through Amazon itself.

Current 2020 Amazon Prime Video deals

Amazon Prime Video’s current free trial is 30 days long. It’s only available for those who haven’t tried Prime before. Occasionally, the company will offer the free trial to existing Amazon customers who haven’t tried Prime in a long time.

College students, however, can get a six-month free trial of Prime, including Prime Video. Plus, they’ll receive exclusive online shopping deals and a discounted rate once the trial’s up: either $6.49/mo. or $59/yr. You’ll need a student email or proof of enrollment to sign up for Prime Student and can reap the rewards for four years or until the date you graduate (if that comes first). 

Discounts for the full Prime service are also available for people who have an EBT or Medicaid card. The fee comes to just $5.99/mo.

What should I do after my Amazon Prime Video trial expires?

When your Prime Video trial comes to an end, you can cancel or upgrade to a paid membership. To get access to just Prime Video and nothing else, you’ll pay $8.99/mo. or $108/yr. Alternatively, you can get the full Prime membership for a slightly higher price: $12.99/mo. or $119/yr. 

Amazon Prime perks

Prime members get plenty of Amazon-related perks, including free two-day shipping, exclusive shopping promos, and unlimited Prime Music streaming. Of course, full Prime members can also enjoy everything Prime Video has to offer. But if you’re not an avid online shopper, you may just want to use Prime Video. If you do, you’ll have access to an on-demand library of thousands of titles from modern times and years gone by. Amazon’s original titles, such as Mr. Robot and The Man in the High Castle, are also yours to view whenever you like. 

Live TV on Amazon Prime Video

If you’re looking to add a little live TV to your Prime Video subscription, you’re free to do so. Unlike most other on-demand streaming services, Prime Video comes with the option to subscribe to well-known networks. While some only let you watch titles on-demand, others make new episodes available at the same time as they air on regular TV. In other words, you can get a live TV experience for much, much less. You can catch certain live sports games on Prime Video too. Amazon has deals with several leagues across the globe, including with the NFL for Thursday Night Football and with the English Premier League. 

Cancelling Amazon Prime Video

It’s clear Prime Video has a lot to offer for a competitive monthly price. But if the service isn’t for you, make sure you cancel before your free trial ends. If you forget, you’ll automatically be charged for a paid membership. To cancel, head to the Your Prime Membership section of your account. There, you’ll see an option to End Trial. Amazon will try and convince you to stay, so you may have to keep clicking End Trial buttons until you reach the cancelation confirmation page. 

Remember, there are plenty of other on-demand streaming services to try. Netflix and Hulu both have equally impressive catalogs with award-winning original content that can’t be viewed elsewhere. Both come with a similar 30-day free trial. (Hulu even has a live TV option that you can try for 7 days.) 

The final cut

From highly-rated original content to live TV add-ons, Prime Video is an all-around good choice for any streaming fan. Its 30-day free trial ensures you’ll have enough time to try out all of the options, whether it’s a brand new on-demand movie, a live NFL game, or a rewatch of your favorite show. So go ahead and try it. What have you got to lose?
